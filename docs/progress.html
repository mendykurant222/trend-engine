⚙️ How the engine works — end to end

The daily pipeline, from raw internet chatter to an early trend call — spotting products and consumer shifts weeks before they go mainstream. Fully automated; every step below is built and running.

Each layer filters the noise of the one above it — what reaches you survived statistics, multiple independent sources, and an AI editor.

1

Collect

Six independent collectors pull raw consumer signals every day: TikTok Creative Center trends, Google rising searches, Reddit communities, Amazon best-sellers, worldwide news volume, and SEC filings.

Under the hood: everything is stored raw with hash de-duplication, so history can always be re-analyzed. A failing source never stops the run.

2

Identify entities

AI reads every new item and names the specific products, brands, and micro-trends inside ("stanley tumbler", "labubu", "dubai chocolate") — while filtering generic noise.

Under the hood: Claude Haiku in batches (~$0.001 per 25 items); spelling variants merge into one canonical entity, and every merge is reversible.

3

Build daily signals

Each entity gets a daily score per source: how often it's mentioned, how much engagement it gets, how much news covers it.

Under the hood: pure SQL, idempotent — any day can be recomputed. The news source arrives with ~90 days of history built in.

4

Detect anomalies

Statistics — not AI — decide what moved unusually today: sudden spikes vs seasonal baselines, week-over-week acceleration, and brand-new entities appearing across multiple platforms at once.

Under the hood: Poisson probability (built for sparse counts), same-weekday baselines, and a ×2 score bonus when 2+ independent sources agree within 14 days — the strongest noise filter.

5

Cluster into trends

AI merges related anomalies into one named trend — a TikTok hashtag, an Amazon product spike, and a Reddit thread about the same thing become a single story with a stage (emerging → accelerating → peak → declining), a strength score, and a separate confidence score.

Under the hood: Claude Sonnet with structured output; a deterministic "trend memory" updates existing trends by entity overlap so nothing gets double-counted day over day.

6

So what? — opportunity lenses

The core output is the discovered trend itself: what's rising, how fast, at what stage, with what evidence — the raw material for product, sourcing, and content decisions before the crowd arrives. On top of that, each trend also gets a public-market lens: which listed companies are exposed (manufacturer / retailer / supplier, long or short, material or not), for when a consumer trend is big enough to touch stocks.

Under the hood: the market lens is a secondary layer — AI proposes, but only tickers verified against the official SEC registry (10,428 companies) are ever stored; the model cannot invent a symbol.

7

Report, alert, self-grade

A daily Telegram report delivers the top trends — stage, momentum, and the evidence behind each; extreme multi-source surges trigger instant alerts; and every Monday the engine grades its own past calls — what it flagged 14 and 30 days ago vs what happened.

Under the hood: every reported trend is logged, so precision is measurable from week one. Watchlisted trends alert at a lower threshold.

8

Dashboard & audit trail

A password-protected web dashboard shows every live trend with its full history — and drills down to the exact posts, searches, and filings behind it. No black box: every claim traces to a raw source item.

Under the hood: every AI prompt, response, and cent of cost is logged to the database. Current all-in running cost: under $1/day.

The thesis: consumer trends surface on TikTok and Reddit weeks before they reach Amazon rankings, news, and earnings calls. This engine watches all of those layers simultaneously, scores the lag statistically, and catches trends while they're still early — then you choose what to do with them: source the product, ride the content wave, or trade the exposure.
